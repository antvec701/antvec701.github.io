About/CV

I am currently a fourth-year economics PhD student at the University of Notre Dame. My primary research objective is to investigate the connections between monetary policy and conditions in the banking sector. I also maintain general interests in macroeconomics, fiscal policy, financial markets, general equilibrium models, and Bayesian econometrics.

I was born and raised in Bismarck, ND. I studied economics and math as an undergrad at the University of St Thomas. I began my doctoral studies at Indiana University and transferred to Notre Dame after my third year.

Beyond economics, I enjoy spending time with friends and family, listening to and playing music, watching basketball and European soccer, graphic design, and hiking. I have a goal of visiting all national parks in the US. You can track my progress by navigating to the "National Parks" tab above.
